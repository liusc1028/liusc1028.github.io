Biography

Sicong Liu studied in Tsinghua University from August, 2008 to July, 2012. He received his B.S.E. in electronic engineering from Tsinghua University, Beijing, China. Currently, he is pursuing the Ph.D. degree, supervised by Prof. Jian Song, in electronic engineering in Tsinghua University. Sicong Liu has published 15 jounal papers and 14 conference papers. He owns 6 Chinese invention patents. He is one of the core members that draft the Broadband Power Line Communications Standard in China. He received the National Scholarship Award for Graduates in both 2014 and 2016, and won the Best Oral Paper Award of the National Doctoral Student Academic Conference. He is the reviewer of IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing, IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Transactions on Broadcasting, IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology and IEEE Transactions on Power Delivery. He is a student member of IEEE.

His research interests lie in sparse signal processing, compressed sensing (CS), interference mitigation, resource allocation, optimization and their applications in the field of smart grid communications, multimedia broadcasting systems as well as the power line communication (PLC) and visible light communication (VLC). Specifically, he mainly focuses on the applications of PLC on smart grid and the integration of PLC and VLC for broadband multimedia access. Besides, he has done plenty of research on the non-Gaussian narrowband interference and impulsive noise recovery and cancellation in broadband transmission systems.
